AEROBIC EXERCISES

For All Ages

DO CARDIO LIKE A PRO. In a nutshell, the term aerobic means "with oxygen." Aerobic exercise and activities are also called cardio, short for "cardiovascular." During aerobic activity, you repeatedly move large muscles in your arms, legs and hips. Your heart rate increases and you breathe faster and more deeply..

BALANCE TRAINING

Why Balance Training Is Important??

It may be the missing component to your workout. Improving your balance can help you avoid falls and stay active when you age. It also helps to improve athletic performance.Performing free weight exercises, such as lunges and deadlifts, can help to strengthen the stabilizer muscles.The beauty of balance training is that anyone can, and should, do it.When you strengthen muscles, it also helps arthritis by giving more support to painful joints.

STRENGTH TRAINING

Be the "MOST FITTEST"!

Modalized Equipments available. The main objective of performing strength training exercise is to increase muscle mass, build strength, stimulate bone growth, improve balance, and reduce the risk of strain or injury to your body.Depending on your goal you can perform exercises like split squat, the kettlebell swing deadlifts,Lifting weights, Using resistance bands, Using your body weight for resistance, by doing push-ups, pull-ups, crunches, leg squats or push-ups against a wall.

FLEXIBILITY TRAINING

Needness to be "Flexibile like a Bamboo"!

Components of Flexibility- muscle elasticity and length, joint structure and nervous system. While heredity controls a person's joint structure, muscle elasticity and length and the nervous system can be positively impacted by regular flexibility training.Most importantly, it improves your performance and reduces the risk of getting injured. Flexibility training is essential in any kind of fitness training. Yoga, butt kick, hip roll are some exercises you can do to increase your muscle flexibility.
